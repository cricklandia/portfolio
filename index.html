About

I was born in Alabama to parents from Georgia and Illinois but raised in Florida from a very young age. After thirteen years in the public school system, I probably ended up falling into the "band nerd" category — though at the time I would have liked to think I wasn't as nerdy as the others, but hindsight is 20-20. Anyway, I continued that theme into college where I majored in music with my principal instrument being classical guitar.

By graduation time, I was teaching private lessons out of a small independent music store and continued that until I married my college sweetheart and realized I needed something more substantial, so I became a state government worker. This was a path many of us who graduated from Florida State University took due to living in the state capital where the majority of jobs are in government. The Florida Department of Education hired me as an IT specialist on an operations team in the Bureau of Educator Certification. It was my first job in the professional world, and I enjoyed it, but after four years there my wife’s father began a life changing battle with cancer which rendered him partially paralyzed and unable to work.

With my in-laws living over four hundred miles away in South Florida, we decided to relocate there to help support her family. Since I had already been in the state government system, I naturally took another state job which was at the child support office in West Palm Beach. After three years of being there for my in-laws, my wife and I realized we preferred our old life in Tallahassee. I was offered my old job back at DOE, so back to North Florida we went.

It was after the return to Tallahassee that the marriage ended, which left me without much of a reason to stay in Tallahassee. I had been with DOE for a combined total of eight years by this point. I had some local friends, but no immediate family, so it became obvious that a change needed to occur, so that’s how I ended up back in my birth state. My strong familial connections in North Alabama along with the area's burgeoning tech industry made it an obvious place to call home. Since moving to Madison, I have completed a second bachelor’s degree at Athens State University where I spent over three years studying programming, problem analysis, problem-solving, and software engineering.

Classroom Experience

For my software engineering capstone project at Athens State in Spring 2022, I developed an application for our customer on top of a preexisting back end which was developed by the Fall '21 team. My team was responsible for designing, implementing, and testing the front end requirements given to us by the customer. With diligent communication with the client and my team, we were able to develop a reliable product which pushed, posted, rendered, and updated data between various user views and established connections between the front and back end. The front end aspects that I worked on were written in Vue.js and plain JavaScript. Other aspects of the project included a Docker container environment consisting of NGINX, Django, and Vue servers. Python was used for terminal commands and scripts to manage the back end as well as to define data tables within the Django REST API. Version control was all done through GitHub using terminal commands and the Visual Studio Code integrated source control.

Other academic projects included:

  • Website redesign for local non-profit literacy organization using Wordpress
  • Used PHP, MySQL, HTML, CSS, and JS for web development
  • Designed and developed database using MySQL Workbench and phpMyAdmin
  • Mobile iOS app dev using Xcode and Swift
  • C++ programs and data structures
