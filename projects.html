AstroPitch

West Palm Beach, Florida
2012 - 2014

At this point, I was a state worker outside the state capital so I was given a small pool of options for employment if I was going to continue my public sector trajectory. Luckily I landed what might end up being the most bizarre job of my career, which was at the Child Support Enforcement office in West Palm Beach. The population diversity of South Florida is unlike anywhere I had ever lived, and the cast of characters I had the pleasure of interviewing (with glass separating us) taught me more about the human condition than anything else up until this point. More often than interviewing clients in an interviewing room, I was seated at my desk, tasked with compiling financial and employment data and creating in house child support orders. These were known as administrative orders as opposed to judicial orders created by the courts.

  • Piloted a cost reducing directive to administer DNA testing by state employees rather than contracting through vendor
  • Interpreted and processed court orders and other legal documents
  • Abided by complex state guidelines to establish child support orders using financial data provided by the client combined with research utilizing DOR resources
  • Coordinated and streamlined process to minimize customer wait times and maximize accuracy of DNA collections process
  • Utilized a task based CRM software based work environment, similar to Jira in the tech world
  • Educated the client regarding the steps to obtain an order
  • Coordinating genetic testing appointments and lab analysis with contracted state vendors and legal service providers
  • Knowledge of state law and policy regarding paternity and child support

Roldoku

Tallahassee, Florida
2007 - 2011

My first time working in the "professional world" was in FLDOE's Bureau of Educator Certification. At that time, men in the bureau were required to wear ties, so I think that's what makes me say "professional". On my first day my supervisor gave me the task of troubleshooting an inexpensive HP inkjet printer. After spending hours on hold with HP customer service, we ended up just boxing it up and shipping it back to be replaced. That headache was how I was introduced to the next ten years of being a state employee. But despite the interesting first day, the job did give me invaluable lessons and experience as shown below.

  • IT support for internal and external stakeholders
  • Provided technical assistance to all internal employees and external stakeholders
  • Assessed and resolved urgent system and technical issues
  • Maintained production statistics and system status reports
  • Technical assistance and troubleshooting of all office PCs, equipment and software utilized by a bureau staff of 60 employees in addition to managing the BEC technical help e-mail inbox which served all school districts in the state
  • Preparing reports on bureau production statistics and technology systems status
  • Investigating, analyzing, and making resolution recommendations for BEC system issues
  • Maintaining all equipment and supply inventory records including: maintenance contracts (renewing and evaluating cost effectiveness), office supplies, and following protocol for property addition, deletion, transfer, and surplus
  • Discovered thousands of dollars in savings by upgrading outdated scanning equipment, made recommendation on purchase which eliminated wasteful spending on maintenance contracts
